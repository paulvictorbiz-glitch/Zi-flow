# 3-Month Video Editing Course (CapCut Pro)

## Overview
- **Duration**: 3 months (12 weeks, ~8–10 hours/week)
- **Level**: Beginner
- **Tool**: CapCut Pro (mobile/desktop)
- **Goals**: Produce polished travel vlogs, cinematic Instagram reels, and YouTube documentaries.
- **Outcomes**: Master cutting, transitions, J/L cuts, B‑roll, speed ramps, stabilization, audio cleanup, basic color correction, sound design, motion graphics, LUTs, pacing/storytelling, and a professional workflow (organization, client revisions, file management).

## Month 1 — Foundations (Weeks 1–4)

### Module 1: Interface & Basic Editing (Week 1)
**Lessons**
- Navigating CapCut Pro: timeline, preview, tools.
- Importing clips, frames/fps/resolution settings.
- Cutting, trimming, splitting, joining clips.
- Adding music, titles, basic transitions.
- Exporting settings (formats, quality, bitrates).

**Learning Objectives**
- Safely import/export and perform non-destructive cuts.
- Apply basic titles and transitions.

**Practical Exercise**
- Edit a 20–30 second clip with music, text, and transitions.

**Milestone**
- Deliver a 30-second "mini edit" for review.

---

### Module 2: Story Structure & Pacing (Week 2)
**Lessons**
- Story beats: hook → journey → climax → resolution.
- Shot selection and order for travel vlogs.
- Basic pacing: short cuts for energy, longer for mood.

**Learning Objectives**
- Build a simple 3-part story arc.

**Practical Exercise**
- Reorder 5 travel clips into a story arc; add music and text.

**Milestone**
- 45-second travel "mini-vlog" draft.

---

### Module 3: Audio Basics & J/L Cuts (Week 3)
**Lessons**
- Volume balancing, mute, ducking.
- J-cuts and L-cuts for smooth audio transitions.
- Basic audio cleanup (noise reduction in CapCut).

**Learning Objectives**
- Apply J/L cuts and balance audio levels.

**Practical Exercise**
- Edit a 40-second clip with J/L cuts and balanced audio.

**Milestone**
- 40-second "audio-smooth" edit.

---

### Module 4: Transitions, Stabilization & Speed (Week 4)
**Lessons**
- CapCut transitions (cross-dissolve, zoom, slide).
- Stabilization tool and how to avoid shaky footage.
- Speed curves: custom ramps, slow/fast motion.

**Learning Objectives**
- Stabilize shaky clips and apply speed ramps.

**Practical Exercise**
- Stabilize 2 clips, add speed ramps, and a transition.

**Milestone**
- 50-second "stabilized + speed" edit.

---

### Month 1 Checkpoint
- Assemble 3 mini-edits (Modules 1–4) into a 1–2 minute "foundations reel".
- Review on: cuts accuracy, pacing, audio balance, stabilization, transitions.

## Month 2 — Intermediate Storytelling & Cinematics (Weeks 5–8)

### Module 5: B‑Roll, Coverage & J/L Cut Mastery (Week 5)
**Lessons**
- B‑roll purpose: context, detail, emotion.
- Coverage strategy: main shot + B‑roll layers.
- J/L cut refinement and multi-layer audio.

**Learning Objectives**
- Build layered shots with B‑roll and refined J/L cuts.

**Practical Exercise**
- Edit a 60-second travel vlog segment with B‑roll and J/L cuts.

**Milestone**
- 60-second travel vlog draft.

---

### Module 6: Color Correction & Basic Grading (Week 6)
**Lessons**
- Exposure, contrast, saturation, white balance.
- Using CapCut’s color tools and LUTs (basic).
- Matching shots for consistent look.

**Learning Objectives**
- Correct exposure and apply basic LUTs for consistency.

**Practical Exercise**
- Correct 5 travel clips and apply a LUT for a cohesive look.

**Milestone**
- 60-second "color-corrected" travel vlog.

---

### Module 7: Sound Design & Layering (Week 7)
**Lessons**
- Layering ambience, music, and Foley.
- Building sound textures (wind, footsteps, city noise).
- Syncing sound to action and pacing.

**Learning Objectives**
- Construct multi-layer sound design for travel scenes.

**Practical Exercise**
- Add ambience + Foley + music to a 50-second clip; sync to action.

**Milestone**
- 50-second "sound-rich" travel edit.

---

### Module 8: Motion Graphics, Text Animation & Masking (Week 8)
**Lessons**
- Text animation presets and custom keyframes.
- Masking for overlays and shape reveals.
- Basic shape graphics and animated location tags.

**Learning Objectives**
- Create animated text and simple masking overlays.

**Practical Exercise**
- Add animated location tags and a masked reveal to a 60-second clip.

**Milestone**
- 60-second "motion graphics" travel vlog.

---

### Month 2 Checkpoint
- Produce a 1.5–2 minute travel vlog integrating B‑roll, color, sound design, and motion graphics.
- Review on story clarity, color consistency, sound layering, and motion graphics timing.

## Month 3 — Advanced Projects & Long-Form Workflow (Weeks 9–12)

### Module 9: Cinematic IG Reels — Editing for Retention (Week 9)
**Lessons**
- Hook-first structure (3–5s hook).
- Rapid cuts, dynamic transitions, and speed ramps for reels.
- On-screen text, captions, and call-to-action.
- Export settings for Instagram (aspect ratio, bitrate, codec).

**Learning Objectives**
- Build a 15–30s cinematic reel optimized for retention.

**Practical Exercise**
- Edit a 20-second travel reel with hook, speed ramps, captions.

**Milestone**
- 20-second cinematic IG reel draft.

---

### Module 10: Documentary Structure & Pacing (Week 10)
**Lessons**
- Documentary beats: intro → problem → journey → resolution.
- Interview editing: J/L cuts, B‑roll, audio cleanup.
- Pacing for long-form: rhythm, silence, and emphasis.

**Learning Objectives**
- Structure a 2–3 minute documentary segment.

**Practical Exercise**
- Edit a 2-minute documentary clip with interview + B‑roll.

**Milestone**
- 2-minute documentary draft.

---

### Module 11: Long-Form Workflow & Organization (Week 11)
**Lessons**
- Project organization: folders, naming conventions, asset management.
- Timeline management for long edits (multi-track, markers).
- Client workflow: revisions, versioning, feedback loops.
- Export presets for YouTube (aspect, bitrate, codec).

**Learning Objectives**
- Manage a long-form project and handle revision cycles.

**Practical Exercise**
- Organize a 3-minute project folder, set markers, and create export presets.

**Milestone**
- Organized 3-minute project ready for review.

---

### Module 12: Final Project — Complicated Long-Form Edit (Week 12)
**Lessons**
- Integrating all skills: story, color, sound, motion, pacing.
- Review and polish: cut redundancy, enhance emotional beats.
- Final export and delivery checklist.

**Learning Objectives**
- Produce a polished 3–5 minute "complicated long-form" piece.

**Final Project**
- Travel documentary or YouTube documentary (3–5 min) with cinematic reels inserted.
- Include hook, interview, B‑roll, color grade, layered sound, motion graphics, and smooth pacing.

**Final Deliverable**
- 3–5 minute long-form documentary + 20-second cinematic reel.

---

### Month 3 Checkpoint & Final Review
- Submit final project + 20-second reel.
- Review on story clarity, color consistency, sound design quality, motion graphics timing, pacing, and long-form workflow.

## Assessment & Rubric

Use this rubric to score each milestone and the final project (0–5 scale):

| Category | 0–1 (Beginner) | 2–3 (Intermediate) | 4–5 (Advanced) |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Cuts & Pacing | Rough, inconsistent timing | Clean cuts, basic pacing | Tight pacing, rhythm matches story |
| Audio & J/L Cuts | Unbalanced, no J/L | Balanced, some J/L | Polished audio, refined J/L cuts |
| Color & Consistency | Overexposed, mismatched | Corrected, consistent LUT | Grade feels cinematic, shots match |
| Sound Design | Single layer, flat | Multi-layer, basic Foley | Rich layers, synced to action/emotion |
| Motion Graphics | Static text | Basic animation | Animated text, masking, overlays |
| Workflow & Organization | Messy folders | Organized, named | Professional workflow, markers, presets |

- **Progress metric**: Average score across modules + final project score.
- **Target**: ≥4.0 average by Month 3 to qualify for "complicated long-form edits".
